Displaying Distributions

A distribution is simply how a bunch of data falls onto a graph.

The level of distribution simply refers to the measures of central tendency, which are often the mean, median, and mode.

The spread of a distribution refers to range, or more specific ranges such as interquartile.

The shape of data refers to what type of graph it is, such as bar, line, or the worst graph in the world, the pie chart.

Outliers in a distribution are easily distinguished as they are visibly separate from the rest of the data. They can have a strong effect on the mean of the data.
